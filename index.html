About

I am a joint Ph.D. student at Northwestern Polytechnical University and Shanghai Artificial Intelligence Laboratory, supervised by Prof. Xuelong Li.

My current work spans open-world visual perception, semantic segmentation, remote sensing and UAV intelligence, time-series modeling for aviation and medical data, and efficient multimodal / vision-language models.

Research

Current research directions reflected by my publications.

Open-World Visual Perception

Object counting, open-vocabulary segmentation, change detection, marine perception, and complex scene understanding.

Remote Sensing & UAV Intelligence

Remote sensing change detection, low-altitude UAV segmentation, crowd localization, and 3D UAV path planning.

Time-Series & Aviation AI

Aircraft sensor anomaly detection, remaining useful life prediction, and state-space models for time-series classification.

Vision-Language & Multimodal Learning

Knowledge distillation for VLMs, multimodal fusion, interpretable text modeling, and vector graphics generation.
